Course Description

This undergraduate course introduces the fundamental principles of digital circuits and logic design, providing students with a strong foundation in the analysis and design of digital systems. Topics include Boolean algebra, combinational and sequential logic design, and the implementation of logic circuits using basic gates and flip-flops.

Through hands-on labs, students will gain practical experience in designing, building, and testing digital circuits using breadboards, logic analyzers, and other tools. The course also explores real-world applications of digital logic in computing and embedded systems, preparing students for advanced studies and careers in electrical and computer engineering.
